BLACKHOLE

Title description, April 2, 2014

Recently nasa has captured an image of the blackhole. A black hole is a region of spacetime exhibiting gravitational acceleration so strong that nothing—no particles or even electromagnetic radiation such as light—can escape from it. The theory of general relativity predicts that a sufficiently compact mass can deform spacetime to form a black hole.

WORMHOLE

Travel through wormholes is possible, but it takes time. A physicist has shown that wormholes can exist: tunnels in curved space-time, connecting two distant places, through which travel is possible. A wormhole (or Einstein–Rosen bridge or Einstein–Rosen wormhole) is a speculative structure linking disparate points in spacetime, and is based on a special solution of the Einstein field equations.
